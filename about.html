About

I work on operating systems, graphics, and compilers.

Now

I spend most of my time on Mesa and the Linux kernel, with occasional upstream work in nearby parts of the stack. That work usually lands in Vulkan drivers, shader compilers, or DRM and platform code.

I care about how software runs on the machine: where data moves, where time goes, and how hardware details shape the result. I prefer straightforward code and explicit data flow.

Work

At Baykar, I initiated the Apache NuttX port for the TI AM67A SoC on the T3 Gemstone platform. I brought up the ARM Cortex-R5 and early boot, including DDR, MPU, cache initialization, and the Linux/U-Boot handoff.

Earlier, I worked on avionics software for a university rocket team, including redundant controllers and a parachute deployment algorithm.

Background

A short path, if you want the background.

More

I ran Minecraft servers, installed custom ROMs, rooted phones, and poked around router interfaces before I properly understood any of it. Around 2015, I also remember modifying a Visual Basic game cheat just to see what would happen.

I started programming more deliberately in 2017, first with C# and Java. In high school, friends and I built small tools and games for each other to try. Around 2019, I kept experimenting with Linux and other small things out of curiosity.

I entered university in 2022 and started learning graphics programming around the same time. Small OpenGL and Vulkan renderers led me toward drivers, compilers, operating systems, and the Linux kernel.
